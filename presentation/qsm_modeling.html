The quantity we want

Magnetic susceptibility — what it is, and why it is worth the trouble of recovering.

The physical quantity

Susceptibility χ: how strongly tissue magnetizes

Place tissue in the scanner's field B0 and it develops its own magnetization M. Susceptibility is the constant of proportionality — a dimensionless tissue property in parts per million.

Definition
M=χH,χ[ppm]

χ is tiny (≈ ±0.2 ppm in brain), so its field effect is subtle — but the MRI phase integrates it over the echo time, making it measurable.

χ > 0 · paramagnetic

Aligns with B0, raises local field. Sources: non-heme iron (ferritin), deoxyhemoglobin, hemorrhage.

χ < 0 · diamagnetic

Opposes B0, lowers local field. Sources: myelin, calcium, lipids.

The sign separates a bleed (+) from a calcification (−) where magnitude cannot.

02

The forward model

From a susceptibility distribution to the phase your scanner records.

Step 1 · χ makes a field

Each susceptibility source is a magnetic dipole

A small region of susceptibility perturbs the field with a dipole pattern — positive lobes along B0, negative to the sides.

Unit dipole kernel (real space)
d(r)= 14π 3cos2θ−1 |r|3

The whole tissue field is every voxel's dipole superimposed — a convolution:

ΔB(r)B0 =d(r)∗χ(r)

Step 1 · in k-space

Convolution becomes a simple multiplication

Fourier-transform both sides and the convolution collapses to a per-frequency product. The kernel has a clean closed form:

Dipole kernel in k-space (B₀ ∥ z)
D(k)= 13− kz2|k|2 ΔB^=B0Dχ^

To recover χ we would just divide by D(k) — trivial, except where it equals zero.

▮ D > 0▮ D < 0— D = 0

Why QSM is hard

The magic angle makes the inversion ill-posed

Kernel vanishes on a cone
D(k)=0 ⟺ kz2|k|2 =13 ⟺ θk=54.7°

On this double cone, susceptibility produces no field at all. Those spatial frequencies are never recorded.

Inverting divides by D. Near the cone D→0, so:

  • noise is amplified without bound
  • missing-cone data → streaking at 54.7°
  • naïve χ^=ΔB^/D is unusable

Regularization, priors and masking all exist to fill this missing cone gracefully.

Step 2 · field becomes phase

The scanner records phase, not field — via Larmor

A field offset shifts precession frequency. Over TE that shift accumulates as measurable phase in the complex signal:

Phase accrual
φ(r,TE)= −γΔB(r)TE +φ0

γ is the gyromagnetic ratio; φ0 is a TE-independent coil offset. The magnitude carries no χ sign — only the phase does. Sign conventions differ by vendor.

WHY PHASE, NOT MAGNITUDE

  • magnitude → anatomy, SNR, R2*
  • phase → the field, and therefore χ
  • phase must be raw & unfiltered — homodyne-filtered SWI phase has the field stripped out
−χ+

Why six echoes

Phase grows linearly with TE — so we fit the slope

Each echo samples the same field at a different TE. A magnitude-weighted fit of φ vs TE gives the field map in Hz — far more robust than one echo.

Your acquisition

6 echoes · TE = 9.8 / 16.8 / 23.8 / 30.8 / 37.8 / 44.8 ms · ΔTE = 7.0 ms

  • six points pin the slope & reject phase noise
  • the offset φ0 drops out of the fit
  • magnitude decay over the same echoes → R2* (iron-sensitive; key to χ-separation)
03

The reconstruction pipeline

Three stages that turn a wrapped phase map into a quantitative χ map.

Stage A · phase unwrapping

Undo the 2π wraps

The scanner records phase only modulo 2π; the true accumulated phase exceeds that and "wraps." Unwrapping restores the continuous field.

φmeas=φtruemod2π

LAPLACIAN

FFT-based Poisson solve — fast, robust, slightly smoothing.

ROMEO your run

Path-based, quality-guided spanning tree weighted by magnitude & gradient coherence. QSMxT default.

Stage B · background field removal

Strip the field from sources outside the brain

The measured field is dominated by background sources — air–tissue interfaces, sinuses, imperfect shim — that dwarf the tissue field.

Decomposition
ftotal=fbackground+flocal

Inside the brain the background is harmonic (∇2fbg=0). A harmonic field equals its spherical mean, so subtracting that mean leaves only the local tissue field.

SHARP / V-SHARP

Spherical-mean-value deconvolution; V-SHARP varies kernel radius near the edge.

PDF your run

Projection onto Dipole Fields — fits background as dipoles outside the mask, then subtracts.

Output: a clean local field — typically <1% of the total.

Stage C · dipole inversion

Deconvolve the dipole — carefully

The naïve solution explodes on the magic cone. Real methods pose it as regularized least squares:

Regularized inversion
χ^= arg minχ 12 ‖W(D∗χ−flocal)‖22 +λR(χ)

W weights by SNR; R(χ) is the prior that fills the missing cone.

MethodPrior R(χ)
TKDthreshold the kernel — fast, streaky
TikhonovL₂ smoothness
MEDIL₁ gradient tied to magnitude edges
TV / RTSedge-preserving total variation

QSMxT default: RTS — an LSMR solve refined with TV, mean-referenced.

What makes a scan QSM-able

Acquisition requirements — and the one trap

  • 3D spoiled multi-echo GRE (T2*-weighted)
  • raw magnitude + phase, per echo — not the combined SWI product
  • phase in true radians, unfiltered (no homodyne high-pass)
  • 4–8 echoes; short first TE; uniform ΔTE
  • monopolar readout preferred (bipolar needs odd/even correction)
  • ~1 mm, near-isotropic; flow compensation helps
  • 3 T floor; 7 T for SNR & small nuclei

The SWIp trap

A scanner's SWI phase is homodyne high-pass filtered: the low-frequency field that background removal and inversion depend on is already gone. No processing recovers it.

This is why the DelRec raw per-echo export is what unlocked χ for this subject.

Reading χ & what it reveals

Interpretation, source separation, and applications in traumatic brain injury.

Interpreting the map

Reading a susceptibility map

χ is quantitative and signed, shown on a diverging colormap centred at zero. Values are relative — referenced to a baseline (whole-brain mean or CSF), since absolute χ has an undetermined constant.

  • + deep gray nuclei (globus pallidus, SN, red nucleus, putamen) — iron
  • + veins — deoxyhemoglobin
  • − white matter — myelin; calcifications

Typical brain tissue spans roughly ±0.2 ppm about the reference.

THE DIAGNOSTIC TRICK

On magnitude or SWI a bleed and a calcification can look identical — both dark. QSM separates them by sign:

+ χ
hemorrhage
− χ
calcification

Beyond net χ

χ-separation: iron and myelin hide inside one number

A voxel holds both signs
χtotal=χpara+χdia

Standard QSM reports only the net. Where iron and myelin coexist they partially cancel — a "normal" χ can mask two opposing pathologies.

Both broaden R2′ regardless of sign, while contributing to χ with opposite signs. Two measurements, two unknowns:

χ (signed) + R2′ = R2* − R2 (unsigned)

  • solve χpara (iron) and χdia (myelin) separately
  • R2* comes free from your six magnitude echoes
  • implemented natively in QSM.rs

Application · traumatic brain injury

What QSM adds in TBI

Microbleeds

Diffuse axonal injury markers. QSM confirms a focus is truly paramagnetic (blood) vs a calcified mimic — the sign SWI can't give.

Deep-gray iron

Chronic TBI accumulates iron in SN, GP, red nucleus, thalamus — a quantitative secondary-neurodegeneration signal.

Oxygenation

Venous χ → OEF / CMRO₂; pairs with ASL CBF for oxygen metabolism after injury.

χ-separation

At a shear lesion: iron deposition and demyelination together — disentangled into separate maps. The strongest research angle here.
